Profile

I'm a 2nd year M.S. student at the Graduate School of Information Science and Technology, The University of Tokyo (since 2019.4), advised by Prof. Masahiko Inami.

Since I was an undergrad, I have been fond of developing a wide range of unique applications, from smartphone apps to hardware prototypes. I often participated in Hackathon (Hacking + Marathon competition) and you can see some of the developed products here (not maintained) and awards (20+) below. My engineering curiosity always looks for novel technologies.

Currently, as a Human-Computer Interaction researcher, I am exploring how computers and humans collaborate together to augment our daily lives. My focus is on developing and evaluating high-impact human-centered systems. I believe interactions through sensing users’ context ubiquitously and intervening them suitably are key for changing our daily lives in a pervasive manner. My recent works are across human behavior analysis, wearable sensing systems, human-AI collaboration, persuasive technologies, and voice conversion. I publish papers at ACM CHI, CSCW, and other venues.
